ABOUT
Kohue Wines produces a limited collection of hand-crafted, high altitude mountain wines from the finest terroirs in the foothills of the Andes in Mendoza, Argentina.

This majestic region offers a unique synergy that nurtures naturally healthy vineyards. The high altitude, distinct soil types, glacial waters, and dry microclimate, allow berries to develop an incredible complexity of aromas and flavors characteristic of the terraces of the Andes.

With minimal intervention, we entrust nature to unveil the identity of each site, preserving the inherent qualities of the fruit. Grounded in years of winemaking experience and a deep respect for nature and its ecosystems, Kohue crafts singular wines that are profound and elegant, offering depth, complexity, and the ability to age beautifully over the decades.

Each year, the Kohue family embarks on a journey between two hemispheres, following the rhythm of the harvest—from Mendoza to Napa. Every bottle tells the story of their journey while capturing the incredible terroir of their native land.

THE NAME
Kohue, pronounced Ko-[weh], is the name of a remarkable native wild plant in the indigenous language, a true protagonist of the flora, fauna, and culture of Mendoza. With its vibrant yellow flowers and captivating aroma, it adorns and perfumes the majestic landscape beneath the Andes.
Chosen and protected as the province’s official plant, it plays a vital role in sustaining our soils and is renowned for its various medicinal properties, discovered by our ancestors.
Just like the plant that inspires our name, Kohue Wines embodies a deep respect for nature, tradition, and sustainability—honoring these values every step of the way.
